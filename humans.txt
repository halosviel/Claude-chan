/* WHAT THIS IS (the human version) */

    Claude-chan is a fully vibe coded virtual "desktop" that runs natively on your
    own machine. You type to her, and she responds in the same little box, an anime girl portrait shows her mood, and she speaks her reply out loud in Japanese. Nothing leaves your computer: there is no API key, no account, no cloud — just the `claude` command-line tool answering locally and a local voice engine doing the talking.

/* HOW TO RUN IT */

    1. Start the voice engine (optional — she just stays silent without it):
         ~/.local/bin/aivisspeech-engine
    2. Start the app:
         python3 server.py      (or press F5 in VS Code)
    3. Open http://localhost:8765 in your browser.

    The `claude` CLI must be installed and logged in — that is what powers the
    conversation. No Anthropic API key is used or needed.

/* HOW IT FITS TOGETHER (no coding required to follow) */

    There are two halves.

    The SERVER (the app/ folder, started by server.py) is a small Python
    program. It hands your browser the page, picks a portrait that matches her
    mood, asks the `claude` tool for a reply, and turns the Japanese line of
    that reply into audio through the voice engine. Each part lives in its own
    file: settings, pictures, voice, the chat itself, and the web plumbing.

    The PAGE (the js/ folder) is what you see and click. It is built from small
    plain-JavaScript pieces — one for the winnewdows you can drag around, one for
    the clock, one for the box you type in, one for her voice, and so on. The
    clever bits (typing the reply out letter by letter, the little chime, the
    "thinking" dots) are written as reusable helpers in js/util/ rather than
    being buried inside one big file.

    The one box on screen is special: it is BOTH where you type AND where her
    answer appears. Press Enter and your text is replaced by her reply as it
    types itself in. You can't type while she is thinking or answering; once she
    finishes, click or press a key and the box is yours again.

/* THINGS THAT ARE INTENTIONAL (not bugs) */

    - Some mood folders under assets/emotions/ are empty on purpose; she falls
      back to a "thinking" picture. These folders are curated by hand — please
      don't auto-fill or overwrite them.
    - If the voice engine isn't running she simply stays silent. That's by
      design; there is no backup voice.
